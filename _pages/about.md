## About

I am a researcher at Inria (Defense & Security Mission) working on speech and audio processing. My current research focuses on the interpretability and explainability of models for Spoken Language Identification (LID) and Automatic Speaker Recognition, with an emphasis on phonetic features.

My background is in experimental phonetics and speech production research. During my PhD at the Laboratoire de Phonétique et de Phonologie (CNRS UMR 7018), Université Sorbonne Nouvelle – Paris 3, I investigated speech production mechanisms with a particular focus on the aerodynamic phase, including subglottal and intraoral pressure.

A central theme of my research has been the description and modeling of aerodynamic parameters involved in speech production. In response to the scarcity of normative data—particularly for French native speakers—I contributed to the development of a reference speech aerodynamics database, provided reference measurements, established detailed measurement protocols, and developed automatic tools for processing and analyzing aerodynamic data.

My doctoral work further examined how these aerodynamic and acoustic parameters vary in pathological speech, particularly in hypokinetic dysarthria associated with Parkinson’s disease.

Overall, my research is structured around a central objective: understanding the mechanisms of speech production and leveraging this knowledge to identify robust and interpretable vocal markers, both for the study of typical and pathological speech and for the development of more reliable and explainable automatic systems for language and speaker recognition.
